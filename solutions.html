There are few easy solutions to your security needs. Major companies with cybersecurity staff get hacked so what chance do you have against malevolent persons? Being aware of the issues and damage limitation are ways to proceed, but first you need to know how your attackers operate and how common scams begin. Update as much and as often as you can and backup on at least 2 different devices (some recommend 3). Use a password safe and generator - avoid passwords like 'Richard_1' or 'fluffy'. If you can think of it so can someone else - do you know what a 'dictionary attack' is? Make a list of all your data assets noting their importance and only keep what is necessary on your machines.

Smartphones: few are aware of the risks involved!

  • Do not respond to telephone or email requests for personal or business information (vishing).
  • With Wifi, do not allow your device to automatically join unfamiliar networks (see Realities phone video).
  • Never send sensitive information over Wifi unless you are absolutely sure it is secure.
  • Disable automatic bluetooth pairing and don't have it on unless needed (bluesnarfing).
  • Only use apps from your mobile provider or registered and well-used sites. Don't download apps from random websites.
  • Watch out for ads, giveaways and contests that seem to good to be true. They often lead to phising sites.
